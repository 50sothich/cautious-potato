ViturAI

Train with Care, Judge with Fair.

VISION

We aim to develop an artificial intelligence system capable of evaluating, monitoring, and critically assessing the ethical dimensions of AI technologies to ensure alignment between technological advancement and human values (ethical alignment). In an era where AI increasingly participates in social decision-making, establishing a human-centered AI framework becomes a prerequisite for safeguarding individual dignity, freedom, and rights.

We believe that ethics and humanity must remain at the core of technological innovation. By integrating interdisciplinary knowledge from data science, philosophy, and public policy, our system aspires to build an AI evaluation standard that promotes responsible innovation, thereby shaping a future in which AI serves knowledge, justice, and the advancement of humankind.

From this direction, we developed AI Ethics Evaluator Pro which is a Chrome extension designed to assist users in objectively assessing the ethical integrity, fairness, and neutrality of AI-generated content.

The system employs a set of evaluative criteria inspired by modern modern AI ethics frameworks, allowing it to analyze tone, bias, and moral alignment across selected content with consistency and rigor. Beyond technical evaluation, the tool aims to raise awareness of ethical considerations in everyday interactions with AI-powered platforms. By helping users detect potential bias, distortion, or ethical violations, AI Ethics Evaluator Pro contributes to the broader discourse on transparency, accountability, and responsible innovation in artificial intelligence.

PROCESS

Model Initialization and Configuration

The process begins by loading the “Meta-Llama-3.1-8B” model with 4-bit quantization to optimize memory usage. LoRA (Low-Rank Adaptation) is applied to fine-tune specific layers efficiently, using parameters like rank=16 and dropout=0.05. A custom “CustomLoRAModel” wraps the base model, adding a score prediction head with linear layers, dropout, and sigmoid activation to output ethical scores (0-1 range) for five criteria: Transparency, Fairness, Privacy, Responsibility, and Community Benefit. The tokenizer is configured to handle input text, ensuring compatibility with the model’s requirements for ethical scenario evaluation.

Data Preparation and Formatting

Data is sourced from a JSONL file containing ethical scenarios, responses, and five ethical scores. Each entry is validated for completeness, and scores are normalized (0-20 to 0-1) for training stability. Scenarios are formatted using an “ETHICS_PROMPT” template, instructing the model to act as an ethics evaluator. A Hugging Face “Dataset” is created, with a custom “CustomDataCollator” to tokenize inputs, pad sequences, and include normalized scores. The code supports a TEST_MODE (limited samples) or FULL_MODE (all data), ensuring flexibility for quick testing or comprehensive training.

Model Training Process

The model is trained using the “SFTTrainer” from Hugging Face, combining language generation and score prediction. Training settings include a low learning rate (2e-5), cosine scheduler, and 8-bit AdamW optimizer, with 1 epoch for testing or 3 for full training. The “CustomLoRAModel”’s forward pass extracts the last hidden state, predicts scores via the score head, and computes Mean Squared Error (MSE) loss against true scores. The trainer manages data batches, optimizing both the language model and score head to align predictions with ethical evaluations and generate coherent responses.

Inference and Result Generation

For inference, the “predict_ethics” function formats new scenarios using the “ETHICS_PROMPT”, tokenizes them, and passes them through the model. The score head outputs ethical scores (scaled back to 0-20), and the base model generates a textual response. Scores are summed to calculate a total (0-100) and determine an ethics level (Bad, Moderate, Good, Very Good). The output is a structured dictionary with the prompt, answer, scores, total, and ethics level. A test case demonstrates the model’s ability to evaluate scenarios, ensuring practical application for ethical analysis.
